\section*{Question 1}
We want to show that homotopic maps are transitive. First of all since $f \sim g$, then there exists a continuous map $H' \colon X \times [0,1] \to Y$ such that $H'(x,0) = f(x)$ and $H'(x,1) = g(x)$, furthermore since $g \sim h$, we have another map $H''$ with similar properties. Therefore defining $H \colon X \times [0,1] \to Y$ such that
\begin{equation}
	H(x,t) = (1-t)H'(x,t) + tH''(x,t) 
\end{equation}
we get a $f \sim h$ since $H$ is continuous and $H(x,0) = H'(x,0) = f(x)$ and $H(x,1) = H''(x,1) = h(x)$.

\section*{Question 2}
We want to show that homotopic equivalences are transitive. Observe that we have some continuous maps that obey the rules of homotopy equivalence
\begin{align*}
	X \overbrace{\longrightarrow}^{f} Y \overbrace{\longrightarrow}^{h} Z \\
	Z \underbrace{\longrightarrow}_{i} Y \underbrace{\longrightarrow}_{g} Y
\end{align*}
By homotopic equivalence, we have the following 
\begin{align*}
	\exists H' \colon Y \times [0,1] \to Y &\text{ continuous such that }\\
	H'(y,0) &= g \circ f(y)\\
	H'(y,1) &= y\\
	\\
	\exists H'' \colon Z \times [0,1] \to Z &\text{ continuous such that }\\
	H''(z,0) &= i \circ h(z)\\
	H''(z,1) &= z
\end{align*}
We claim that there is a homotopy equivalence $X \approx Z$ with the continuous maps $h \circ f \colon X \to Z$ and $g \circ i \colon Z \to X$

		H''(z,2t-1) & t \in [0.5,1]
	\end{cases}
\end{equation}
