Dread

Sometimes at night, a soothing figure of the one I loved is given birth from the lingering silence, formed by the freedom that I was going to taste, I imagined that it was her offering it to me. I wanted to give everything to it, my own heat, my body. Suddenly, I awaken. And the faint memory of the girl I met before dreaming fades away.

When you wake up, your spirit searches vainly for where you are, where were you when you fell asleep. The furniture wakes up with you, it’s immobility is imposed by you, and it’s memory is formed by a sleeper who felt too alone at night admist the slumbering chairs, doors and tables. You try to pinpoint where your limbs are after a swirling night of darkess, trying to guess it’s orientation, it’s existence. Mornings cannot be less dreadful.

I take sleeping pills at night, wake up before my sleep is restful enough, only to find myself unable to fall back asleep, and I drink coffee to stay awake during the day.
