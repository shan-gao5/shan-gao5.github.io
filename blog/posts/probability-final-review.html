Uniqueness and existence of Conditional expectation

This is simple, let $\mathcal{G}$ be a sub-$\sigma$-algebra, then define the measures on $(\Omega, \mathcal{G})$ $$ \mu_{\mathcal{G}}^X(A) = \int_{A} X d\mathbb{P}\quad \forall A \in \mathcal{G} $$ and the measure $$\forall A \in \mathcal{G} \quad \mathbb{P}{\mathcal{G}} = \mathbb{P}[A]$$ which is $\mathbb{P}$ restricted to $\mathcal{G}$ and undefined for all other sets. Obviously $\mu{\mathcal{G}}^X << \mathbb{P}{\mathcal{G}}$, then there exists a unique measurable function called $Y \colon \mathcal{G} \to \mathcal{B}(\mathbb{R})$ such that for all $A \in \mathcal{G}$ $$ \int{A} X d\mathbb{P} = \mu_{\mathcal{G}}^X(A) = \int_{A} Y d\mathbb{P}_{\mathcal{G}} $$

Basic Properties of conditional expectation

  • Pulling out independent factors If $\sigma(X)$ is independent of $\mathcal{G}$, then $$ \mathbb{E}[X | \mathcal{G}] = \mathbb{E}[X] \text{ a.s} $$

  • Double independence $X$ is independent of $(\mathcal{H}, \sigma(Y))$, then $$ \mathbb{E}[XY | \mathcal{H}] = \mathbb{E}[X]\mathbb{E}[Y | \mathcal{H}] $$ Note that this is very different from $X$ being independent of $\mathcal{H}$ and independent of $X$.

  • Bigger sigma alg doesn't matter $$ \mathbb{E}[\mathbb{E}[X | \mathcal{H}] | \mathcal{G}[ = \mathbb{E}[X| \mathcal{H}] $$

  • (Tower property) $$ \mathbb{E}[\mathbb{E}[X | \mathcal{G}] | \mathcal{H}] = \mathbb{E}[X | \mathcal{H}] $$ basically select the smallest $\sigma$-algebra

  • Pulling out known factors If $X$ is $\mathcal{G}/\mathcal{B}(\mathbb{R})$-measurable then $$ \mathbb{E}[X | \mathcal{G}] = X $$ Several results coming from this one, first one is $$ \mathbb{E}[\mathbb{E}[X | \mathcal{G}] | {\emptyset,\Omega}] = \mathbb{E}[X] \ \text{a.s} $$ This is because every $\sigma$-algebra is independent to the trivial $\sigma$-algebra (can check this): thus $$ \mathbb{E}[X | {\Omega,\emptyset}] = \mathbb{E}[X] $$ Also any random variable measurable to ${\Omega, \emptyset}$ is constant almost surely

Another one is that if some $Z$ is $\mathcal{H}$ measurable, then $$ \mathbb{E}[[\mathbb{E}[X | \mathcal{H}] | Z]] = \mathbb{E}[X|Z] $$ We have some other funny properties combining the above

$$ \mathbb{E}[X | \mathbb{E}[X | \mathcal{H}]] = \mathbb{E}[\mathbb{E}[X | \mathcal{H}] | \mathbb{E}[X | \mathcal{H}]] = \mathbb{E}[X | \mathcal{H}] $$

  • Linearity, positivity and monotonicity all hold for conditional expectation

  • cMCT Assume ${X_n}, X \in L^1(d\mathbb{P})$ $X_n \uparrow X$ then $\mathbb{E}[X_n | \mathcal{H}] \uparrow \mathbb{E}[X | \mathcal{H}]$

  • cMCT$^\prime$ $X_n \downarrow X$ then $\mathbb{E}[X_n |\mathcal{H}]\downarrow \mathbb{E}[X|\mathcal{H}]$

  • cDominated Convergence Theorem If $X_n \to X$ a.s or in prob, and $|X_n| \leq Y$ with $Y \in L^1(d\mathbb{P})$, then $\mathbb{E}[X_n | \mathcal{H}] \to \mathbb{E}[X | \mathcal{H}]$ almost surely and in $L^1 d(\mathbb{P})$

  • Fatou's Lemma If $\exists Y \in L^1(d\mathbb{P})$, where $X_n \geq Y$ for all $n \geq 1$, then $$ \mathbb{E}[\liminf_{n \to \infty} X_n | \mathcal{H}] \leq \liminf_{n \to \infty}\mathbb{E}[X_n | \mathcal{H}] $$

  • Reverse Fatou's If $\exists Y \in L^1(d\mathbb{P})$ where $X_n \leq Y$ for all $n \geq 1$, then $$ \limsup_{n \to \infty} \mathbb{E}[X_n | \mathcal{H}] \leq \mathbb{E}[\limsup_{n \to \infty} X_n | \mathcal{H}] $$

  • Holder's for conditional expecation $$ \mathbb{E}[XY | \mathcal{G}] \leq \mathbb{E}[|XY| | \mathcal{G}] \leq (\mathbb{E}[|X|^{p} | \mathcal{G}])^{1/p} (\mathbb{E}[|Y|^q|\mathcal{G}])^{1/q} $$
