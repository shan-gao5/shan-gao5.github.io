On Solitude

Solitude is sometimes not easy to bear. I'd gladly exchange it for your company. It grows like sadness at the beginning of Spring. But perhaps that growth is good. Going into oneself and not meeting anyone for days. It's like the loneliness of a child in a grown ups world, where they bickered about things which seemed grave and beyond my reach. In some ways, I am truly a child in a world where young minds burn at twilight, where light goes to die, where darkness never truly rises. I wish that one day, I will experience something that can make me realize that sorrow and sadness are meager feelings and that "grown-ups" are sometimes truly barren. Until then, I continue to look into the world as a child, sinking into the depth of my own world. To put it simply, my naivety and my uncalculating mind is my biggest insecurity. I wish to communicate  that to you. You may do as you wish with it, discard it, fuel it, dispell it, and, as a shameless hope that I bear, perhaps you may seek to comfort it.  I lay floating on a vast sea and you may control the tides as you wish. 

On love

You've left me, necessarily, and rightly so, in solitude. In this state of mind, I will write about love. 

Love is good. It is also hard. Love between two people, a relationship sprinkled with a bit of wonder that makes it, suddendly, incomparable to any other human experience. It is perhaps the hardest thing that was laid upon me to do, the ultimate test, for which I've prepared a long time. Nevertheless, I am a beginner. I don't know how to love: I must learn. I wish to take in lessons on love with all my strength, my being, displaying the strongest passion with my beating heart. It's a good opportunity for me to mature and to become someone for your sake. It singles out my flaws, and subjects me to vast designs. The lingering possibility that I might be unsufficient for this kind of love, undeniably, frightens me to death. Sometimes I feel the heavy feeling of having failed at it. I realize that love isn't about conventions, it isn't risk free, it isn't a past time, it is all but trivial. The results of love are the culmination for which a human life may be used, and may not even  last sufficiently enough.
